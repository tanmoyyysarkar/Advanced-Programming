1. Why race condition occurs:
Ans. counter++ is not an atomic operation. It involves read, modify, and write steps. When multiple threads perform these steps simultaneously, updates can overlap and some increments are lost, producing incorrect output.

2. How mutex solves it:
Ans. A mutex enforces mutual exclusion for the critical section (counter update). Only one thread can modify the shared counter at a time, so no updates are lost and the final value becomes correct and deterministic.
